Consoulium'24:

The Happiness Festival

Consoulium'24 is the eagerly anticipated annual literary and cultural festival, meticulously crafted under the auspices of the Students’ Association of Bukhari Islamic Da'wa College (SABIC). This four-day festival is a cognitive explosion, showcasing the innate talents of students through a vibrant program of language conferences, lecture series, seminars, summits, exhibitions, book reviews, thought-provoking talks, and dynamic debates. Designed to be an immersive and transformative experience, Consoulium fosters a deeper understanding, encourages self-realization, and imparts timeless wisdom that participants carry with them long after the festival ends.

Theme Note

Festival Overview

The theme of this year’s Consoulium, invites students to explore the essence of true happiness and fulfillment from an Islamic perspective. Amid a world where superficial pleasures are often mistaken for happiness, this theme brings forth the spiritual joy rooted in faith, relationships, and purposeful living.

For a believer, eternal happiness lies in being close to Allah. This festival emphasizes how the greatest joy is reserved for those who are steadfast in their devotion. Across time, humans have pursued pleasure in various forms, yet often this pursuit leaves a void, especially when it focuses solely on temporary delights. Consoulium'24 highlights how the thrill of short-lived pleasures often leads to lasting tensions and unrest.

In a world where the search for happiness is ever-intensifying, Consoulium'24 explores the reasons why our generation is drawn to fleeting pleasures in gaming, social media, and virtual realities, and examines how these pursuits often leave us feeling more disconnected. The festival sheds light on the essence of happiness found in worship, meaningful relationships, and compassionate connections. It reminds students that happiness is not found in harming or isolating others, but rather in lifting each other up, holding each other together, and fulfilling the teachings of Islam. The festival seeks to offer perspectives on the tragic outcomes, such as suicide, that can arise when people chase the illusion of happiness as defined by modern materialism. By grounding happiness in spiritual and moral values, Consoulium offers a holistic view that celebrates life’s deeper, enduring joys.

Purpose

Through Consoulium, SABIC aims to create a space where students can critically and spiritually engage with their concepts of happiness, encouraging them to seek fulfillment beyond the superficial allurements of modernity. It reinforces that the pursuit of happiness aligned with Islamic principles can transform not only personal lives but also foster a more harmonious society.

Event
Structure

This year’s festival, "The Happiness Festival," is not just an event but a movement, inspiring young minds to redefine happiness in a way that promotes both individual well-being and community cohesion. Consoulium'24 calls students to take part in this journey of rediscovery, emerging from it with a renewed, faith-centered perspective on joy, purpose, and fulfillment.

Teams and Symbolism

Consoulium'24, The Happiness Festival is organized around five distinct teams, each embodying a unique facet of spiritual joy and fulfillment in Islam. These teams symbolize sacred concepts that deepen the festival's theme of true happiness.

HIRA-HEIGHTS

“Hira-Heights: Ascend to Spiritual Bliss”

Hira refers to the Cave of Hira, where Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received the first divine revelation, marking the start of his prophetic mission. "Heights" symbolizes spiritual elevation and the culmination of divine joy. Hira-Heights signifies the profound bliss and elevation associated with that pivotal moment in Islamic history.
Muhammad Ansar Parappangadi
Abdul Basith Madadvoor
LIQA-LEAVES

“Liqa-Leaves: Embrace the Joy of Divine Meeting”

Liqa is an Arabic term for the ultimate joy of meeting Allah, marking the pinnacle of a believer’s spiritual journey. Leaves symbolizes beauty, growth, and the spreading of joy, like petals unfolding in divine presence. Liqa-Leaves signifies the spiritual fulfillment of meeting Allah, intertwined with the imagery of growth and the spreading of this sacred joy.
Muhammed Sinan Aloor
Savad Kolappuram
Shafa-Lights

“Shafa-Lights: Illuminating the Path to Divine Intercession”

Derived from Shafa'a, meaning intercession in Islam, it refers to the Prophet Muhammad’s (PBUH) intercession on the Day of Judgment. The term ‘Lights’ symbolizes the spiritual illumination and joy this intercession brings. Shafa-Lights represents the profound relief and joy believers will experience through the Prophet's intercession.
Muhammad Nafih Kakkadampuram
Zakariya Kannur
Sidra-Sights

“Sidra-Sights: Ascend to Divine Realities”

Sidra refers to Sidratul Muntaha, the sacred tree symbolizing the Prophet's nearness to Allah during the Mi’raj. Sights signifies the Prophet's vision of divine realities during this ascension. Sidra-Sights represents the exalted moment of spiritual elevation, where the joy of closeness to Allah is realized through the gift of prayer (Salat).
Jabir Kollam
Junaid Chemmala
HOUZE-DEWS

“Spiritual Renewal: The Bliss of Houze-Dews”

Houze, A transliteration of Hawl (Arabic) or Houz (Urdu), derived from Hawl al-Kawsar, the fountain of abundance in paradise, symbolizes the Prophet’s offering to his followers. "Dews" represents drops of moisture, evoking relief and renewal. Houze-Dews embodies the soothing drink from Hawl al-Kawsar, offering eternal refreshment and bliss to the Ummah on the Day of Judgment.
Muhammad Abdu Rahman Vadakkangara
Munees Kuzhippuram
