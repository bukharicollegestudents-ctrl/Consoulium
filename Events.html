Happiness Hora Visit

2024

The Happiness Hora Visit brought Consoulium’24 to life across 12 campuses, inviting affiliated institutions to participate in the festival’s events and competitions. Representatives from SABIC visited these campuses, extending personal invitations and sharing program details with students eager to join in the celebrations. This outreach strengthened the bonds among the college communities, bringing the spirit of Consoulium to campuses across the region and uniting students in the shared pursuit of knowledge, creativity, and joy.

Kulavi Podcast

Youtube Channel

The Kulavi Podcast series brings students into meaningful discussions on pressing social and ethical issues, delving into Islamic perspectives on topics relevant to the modern world. Leveraging the growing influence of podcasts as a medium, students use this platform as a contemporary tool for Islamic propagation, effectively reaching audiences in ways that resonate with today’s digital generation. Each episode offers insights into how Islamic teachings apply to current challenges, making it a powerful bridge between tradition and modern discourse.

Podcast Competition

Deadline: 2024

This all-Kerala podcast competition invites teams from colleges across the state to showcase their skills in content creation, Islamic thought, and digital media. Students from various institutions compete to create impactful podcasts, discussing themes relevant to the Muslim community and society at large. Winners of the competition were recognized for their creativity, insight, and effectiveness in communicating Islamic perspectives, with top teams awarded cash prizes to encourage further exploration in this medium.

Ashaya Samvadham

16 NOV 2024

Ashaya Samvadham is a dynamic forum for debate, encouraging students to examine and discuss controversial Islamic topics with depth and respect. Organized around the principles of Ahlussunnathi Wal Jama’a, this event allows participants to challenge each other with questions, debate differing viewpoints, and collaboratively work toward resolutions. By fostering respectful, informed discussion, Ashaya Samvadham cultivates critical thinking and provides a model for handling ideological differences within an Islamic framework.

Academic Conference

17 NOV 2024

This academic event featured presentations on several research papers exploring the rich cultural diversity among Indian Muslims in rituals, worship practices, and community traditions. By highlighting these variations, the presentation aimed to showcase the pluralism within the Indian Muslim identity, discussing themes that span historical, regional, and social contexts. This event allows students to appreciate the unity in diversity among Indian Muslims, providing a nuanced understanding of cultural practices across the country.
